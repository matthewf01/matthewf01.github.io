Unit 3 Study Guide

Judicial Branch (AKS 46)

Highest Court: Georgia's Supreme Court

Supreme Court's Role: To determine if actions are constitutional and interpret laws.

Justice Elections: Justices are elected in a state-wide election for a 6-year term.

Main Roles of Courts: 1) Administer justice, 2) Prosecute criminals, 3) Resolve civil disputes impartially.

Types of Law:
  • Civil: Disputes between people (e.g., lawsuits).
  • Criminal: Deals with violations of the law (felonies and misdemeanors).
Adult Criminal Justice Steps:
  1. Arrest
  2. Booking
  3. Hearing (formal word is Indicting)
  4. Trial
  5. Sentencing
  6. Appeal (if a fair trial is questioned)

Juvenile Justice (AKS 47)

Who is a Juvenile? Anyone under 17 years old.

Goal of Court: To protect and rehabilitate.

Types of Acts:
  • Delinquent: A crime if done by an adult (e.g., theft).
  • Unruly (Status Offense): Only wrong for a juvenile (e.g., skipping school).

No Juries: The judge makes the decisions.

Juvenile Justice Steps:
  1. Intake: Taken into custody, parents notified.
  2. Released or Detained: Released to parents or held (detained) at a YDC if there is enough evidence.
  3. Informal Adjustment: Optional for first-timers who admit guilt; supervised for 90 days.
  4. Adjudicatory Hearing: Judge determines guilt or innocence.
  5. Dispositional Hearing: Judge hears witnesses and determines consequences.
  6. Sentencing: Judge rules on consequences (e.g., boot camp, probation, fines).
  7. Appeal: Can appeal if there is evidence of innocence.

Local Government (AKS 48)

Main Types:
  • City (Municipality): Provides services like police, trash pickup. Established by a Municipal Charter.
  • County: Carries out state programs (e.g., courts). Mandated by the GA Constitution.
  • Special-Purpose: Has one specific job (e.g., school district, MARTA). Created to benefit citizens.
How They're Funded:
  • Sales Taxes (like SPLOST)
  • Ad Valorem (Property) Taxes
  • User Fees and Fines
